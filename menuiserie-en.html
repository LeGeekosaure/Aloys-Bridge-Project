The Plan

Before starting, you need to draw!

To do carpentry, you always need to have a plan first to know what to do.

Material Presentation

Before starting, you need to prepare:

- The wood

First, you need to choose the type of wood according to the needs, such as strength or weight. We chose a pine board, which has a satisfactory resistance for its mass, quite advantageous for a competition where every gram counts.

- The tools

After choosing the wood, we need to prepare the tools. For this kind of work, we will use simple tools such as a DIY table saw that can cut precise straight cuts with angles from 90 to 45Â°, an ideal choice for this task. We also need more manual tools like hammers, nails, and glue for assembly.

- The workspace

And for the grand finale, the workspace, the "holy bench," the place where all the basic work is done and where everything is always (well, usually) organized.

The Cutting

(The most fun part!)

The first step in woodworking (after measuring) is cutting, which is pretty cool and dangerous.

The Assembly

(This part is not fun)

Using angle clamps to make sure the slats are square.

Using clamps to let the glue rest.

This is a very important step in woodworking. Assembly, where precision is key, and where we finally see the result of our creation. The materials used are nails and glue.

The Result

(Almost final)

Here is the raw result. The real finish will be the painting and fixation. And we have reached the end of the journey of an apprentice carpenter. Shoutout to Enzo's grandfather who helped us a lot in the making of this piece.
